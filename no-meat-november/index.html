THE CHALLENGE

No Meat November invites you to participate in a simple challenge.
Can you go a month without eating meat? We believe in 2017 it is easier than ever to make this
change, and we want to equip you with the necessary tools to help you complete the full 30
days. We’re even convinced that many of you will stick with your new diet in the long term and
we hope you will invite others to take the 30-day challenge as well.

Interested? Keep reading...

Why Cut Out Meat?

Animal welfare

  • Everyday, 2.5 million farm animals are slaughtered for meat in the UK
    alone
  • 70 billion farmed animals are killed globally every year
  • There are 800 US-style mega farms operating across the UK
  • 70% of pigs reared in the UK are farmed intensively
  • Animals are sentient beings – they experience pain both physical and mental. Can slaughter ever be humane?

Environment

  • Animal agriculture is responsible for 18 % of greenhouse gas
    emissions, more than the combined exhaust from all transportation
    across the world
  • Methane has a global warming potential 86 times that of CO2 on a 20
    year time frame
  • Animal agriculture is the leading cause of species extinction, ocean
    dead zones, water pollution, and habitat destruction
  • In the amazon alone 1-2 acres of rainforest are cleared every second
    and animal agriculture is responsible for 91% of this
  • The amount of water required to produce one quarter-pounder beef
    burger is around 3000 litres

Personal Health

  • The largest study ever conducted in the UK (EPIC-Oxford study)
    comparing rates of heart disease between vegetarians and non-
    vegetarians found that vegetarianism can reduce the risk of heart
    disease by 32%
  • This study also showed that meat eaters had a 47% increased risk of
    heart disease
  • An Adventist Health Study in 2012 showed a clear association between
    vegetarianism and a lower risk of cancers
  • Most studies also agree on the association between a vegetarian diet
    and diabetes, with vegetarians having lower rates of diabetes. A large
    study showed that the risk of diabetes was about twice as high for non-
    vegetarians as for vegetarians

What is the 30-day challenge all about?

Challenging yourself and your friends

Chances are you’ve thought about trying vegetarianism before. Well now is the time to take the challenge and why not ask a friend/housemate/family member to do it with you too.

Raising awareness

Don’t just make a pledge in silence. Tell people why you’re doing it and hit them with the facts. Together we’ll make a difference.

Realising the benefits

You’ll be amazed how much you save financially as well as feeling fitter and healthier. We’ll also be counting how many equivalent animal lives we’ve spared and litres of water we’ve conserved.
